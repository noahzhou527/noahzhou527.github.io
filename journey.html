Journey Map

From hesitation to session momentum

This journey follows Alex, a beginner climber, through one gym visit and highlights the moments where CRAG can reduce friction and increase motivation.

"I want to climb more, but sometimes I just stand there and wonder which route I should even try."
- beginner climber mindset represented in our primary persona
Stage Action Thoughts / Feelings Pain Point CRAG Opportunity
Before arriving
 Decides whether to go climbing after class. Interested, but not fully motivated. No session goal. Climbing alone feels easier to cancel. Route Quest suggests a simple challenge for the day.
Entering the gym
 Looks around the wall and scans available routes. Curious but slightly intimidated. Experienced climbers make the space feel harder to enter. The app recommends beginner-friendly problems with less guesswork.
Choosing a problem
 Picks a route based on color and appearance. Unsure if the route matches his skill level. Cannot easily tell the intended flow or strategy. Beta Buddy provides short hints instead of overwhelming instruction.
Attempting the climb
 Falls at the same move a few times. Frustrated and self-conscious. No feedback loop, so failure feels random. Hints and community tips help him adjust without embarrassment.
After the session
 Leaves the gym and tries to remember what happened. Not sure whether the session counts as progress. Attempts and improvements feel invisible. Route Quest logs attempts, badges, and streaks.
Thinking about next time
 Considers coming back later in the week. Would feel more confident with a partner. Social barrier reduces consistency. Climb Circle makes it easier to join a session or meet someone at a similar level.
Pain Point 01

Route uncertainty

Beginners often cannot read the route flow, which makes the first attempt feel confusing rather than exciting.

Pain Point 02

Invisible progress

Without small markers of growth, users may leave a session feeling like they only failed.

Pain Point 03

Low social confidence

Many users are more likely to return if they feel connected to people, tips, or a sense of community.
