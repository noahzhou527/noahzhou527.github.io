Playful Features

Core features for a more guided, playful, and social climbing journey

The concept is built around three core layers: Guided Progress Quests, Climb Circles, and Exploration-Based Discovery. Together they turn isolated sessions into a connected climbing experience.

Feature 01

Guided Progress Quests

Turns personal climbing goals into small, achievable milestones so each session feels like part of a meaningful progression loop.

  • Complete quests such as climbing 3 routes above last week's comfort grade
  • Revisit saved projects and improve attempt quality over time
  • Supports self-efficacy through concrete, trackable progress markers
Feature 02

Climb Circles

Lets users create or join small friend groups for short, motivating challenges without the pressure of public competition.

  • Set goals like 2 sessions this week or finish a beginner route set together
  • Share small contributions such as one beta tip or one completed challenge
  • Adds social motivation without relying on public leaderboards
Feature 03

Exploration-Based Discovery

Rewards exploration behaviours so playfulness is tied to participation, discovery, and community involvement instead of only performance.

  • Unlock badges for joining a beginner session or attending a new gym event
  • Earn map-style markers for climbing with new partners or trying new wall areas
  • Encourages broader engagement across the climbing community

Why these features

Each layer responds to a different human need

Together, these three core features create a system where progress, discovery, and social motivation reinforce one another instead of existing as separate tools.

Progress Visibility

Guided Progress Quests

Makes climbing improvement easier to see by breaking long-term goals into specific session-to-session milestones.

Social Motivation

Climb Circles

Builds accountability and encouragement through small-group challenges that feel friendly rather than competitive.

Community Discovery

Exploration-Based Discovery

Encourages users to explore events, spaces, and people in the climbing community instead of staying in a narrow routine.
